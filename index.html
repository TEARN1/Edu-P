Section A: Comprehension

  • A text passage (e.g., an article, speech, or short story) will be provided.
  • You'll be asked questions based on the text, testing your understanding of vocabulary, context, author's purpose, and language use.

    • Literal Comprehension
    These questions test your understanding of the explicit information presented in the text. They often require you to identify facts, details, or main ideas. Example: Who is the main character in the story?
    Evaluative Comprehension
    These questions ask you to analyze the text and form your own opinions about it.
    Inferential Comprehension
    These questions require you to read between the lines and draw conclusions based on the information provided. Example: What can you infer about the character's feelings based on their actions?
    Vocabulary
    These questions test your understanding of words used in the text. Example: What is the meaning of the word "elucidate" as used in the passage?

    Section B: Summary

  • A passage will be given, and you'll need to summarize the main points in your own words within a specified word limit.
  • This section tests your ability to identify key information and condense it effectively.

    • Key Skills Tested:

    Identifying main ideas


    You need to determine the core points of the text.

    Conciseness


    You must express the key information in as few words as possible.

    Clarity


    Your summary should be easy to understand.

    Objectivity


    You should avoid expressing personal opinions or adding new information.

    Common Requirements:

    Word limit


    The summary must be within a specified number of words.

    Format


    You might be asked to write in paragraph form or point form.

    Use of own words


    You should express the information in your own words, avoiding plagiarism.

    Section C: Language

  • This section focuses on language structures and conventions.
  • You might encounter questions on:
    - Vocabulary and word usage
    - Grammar and sentence structure
    - Editing and correction
    - Language variation and registers

    • Common Question Types

    Grammar and syntax
    Identifying and correcting errors in sentence structure, verb tense, subject-verb agreement, etc.

    Vocabulary
    Using the correct word in context, understanding synonyms and antonyms.

    Punctuation
    Applying punctuation marks correctly (commas, periods, apostrophes, etc.).

    Spelling
    Correcting spelling errors.

    Language variation
    Understanding different language registers (formal, informal, etc.) and using them appropriately.

    Editing and proofreading
    Identifying and correcting errors in a given text.
